The conflict has elevated regional tensions, with reports of ballistic missile interceptions over Saudi Arabia and strikes on Bahrain.

By March 7, the market prices the probability of a US/Israel strike on March 7 at 100¢ (100%), with $2.47M volume, and the "US strikes Iran by March 31" market also shows 100% certainty ($22.2M volume). Overnight (March 6–7), the March 7 contract maintained its 100% price, but volume surged to $2.47M from $1.0M the prior day, signaling sustained conviction. All date-specific markets from March 1 onward now resolve "Yes" as strikes continue daily.

## Catalysts

The primary catalyst was the initiation of US-Israeli strikes on February 28/March 1, which shattered pre-war uncertainty. The rapid escalation included decapitation strikes against Iranian leadership, nuclear facilities, and military targets, as reported by Al Jazeera and BBC. Political statements from Trump and Hegseth reinforced the intent to intensify the campaign, driving market participants to price in prolonged hostilities. The unprecedented $500M+ wagering (CoinDesk, March 1) reflected global attention and belief that strikes would persist. Additionally, the reported involvement of France in hosting US aircraft (Times of India) broadened the coalition, further entrenching the conflict.
